UX/UI Design, Case Study

Art Finder

An app to efficiently deliver information about art events and exhibitions,
facilitating a smooth support process
from discovering events of interest to actually attending them.

Prototype

Background

The goal is to empower users to efficiently and regularly access information about art.
The primary targets are:

  • Due to weekday work, those who primarily desire to attend weekend events.
  • Professionals such as designers, who frequently use PCs and prefer physical events over online ones during the weekends.
  • Individuals with specific genre interests, but are also open to new and recommended experiences.

Research

Conducted a survey on existing competing apps,
extracting the positive aspects and points of concern for each.
From this analysis, obtained the following insights:

  • Users prefer local information over international or city-based details.
  • A single, well-categorised filter per screen streamlines event searches.
  • The following elements could be factors for making a decision to attend:
    appealing images, the events access, duration, and popularity.
  • Some users would prefer an easy way to browse specific art genres.
  • It might be convenient to connect to users phone's Calendar function.
  • The save events function should be easy to use.
  • A simple colour tone may clash less with the art event images.

User Interview

Conducted user interviews with 5 individuals to delve into insights.
From these interviews, discovered:

  • Users generally prefer events that are closer in proximity.
  • Many users tend to use mapping applications like Google Maps when planning to attend an event.
  • Additional decision-making factors include the popularity of the event and whether it features a favourite artist.
  • The use of genre categorisation varies among users.
  • A lot of users utilise the app's calendar function.

User Journey / Wireframes

Created the user journey and wireframes for the app.
With specific considerations based on the interview results:

  • Default setting displays nearby events, enabling users to refine preferences.
  • Users can search events based on their current location using a map display.
  • Home screen prominently features popular, recommended, and latest information.
  • Follow favourite galleries and artists for real-time updates.
  • Save interesting events for easy access and view locations seamlessly on maps.
  • Calendar sync for added convenience.

Prototype

Designed fundamental aspects of the app such as fonts and colours.
Ensured buttons are prominent while maintaining a simple, white-based design that complements various art images effectively.

Usability Test

Using Maze, Conducted usability tests with 6 individuals to evaluate the user journey (1 discovere an event → 2 save it → 3 check its access).
Participants also provided a 5-point rating on factors such as colour and layout visibility.
Despite some non-functional search features due to prototype, the overall feedback received was highly positive.

A/B Test

After reviewing the heatmaps from the usability test,
noticed that users tend to focus on the initial view of the exhibition's detail page to check for access.
To streamline the process, added the access link within the first view.

Conducted an A/B test to evaluate the difference.
4 out of 5 people found the proposed improvement easier to understand.
