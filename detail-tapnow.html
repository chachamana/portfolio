UX/UI Design, Visual Design, Branding

Tapnow

An app for generation Z that enables users to send photos to friends' home screens.
Contributed improvements to entire UX/UI design by Figma, Also created ads for multi languages.
Achieved 1 million downloads and 1.3 million posts per month since I joined.

Example 01

PR at App Store

Find Problem

Revised TapNow's visuals on the App store, which serve as users' initial entry point.
The following requests were raised:

  • Clearly conveying the app sends photos to recipient's widget
  • Highlighting the App as enjoyable with friends
  • Visual Design tailored to the target generation

Research

Conducted research on competing companies, apps popular among Generation Z, and widget-related applications to investigate visual representations.

Design Proposal

Based on the research, created layouts emphasising the expression of sending to widgets.
Additionally, proposed variations such as using the main colour with the same bright tones and monochrome patterns.

Revamp Design

A pattern emphasising a simpler and cooler impression
was adopted as the overall direction for the future.
The day after the revamp, it caught the attention of
a prominent YouTuber and went viral, resulting in the app
securing No,1 on the Free apps chart (Aug 2022).
This contributed significantly to user acquisition.

Example 02

Permission Rate

Find Problem

Optimised Tapnow's onboarding process to increase the app's notification permission rate.

The following requests were raised:

  • Changing the form of presenting pop-ups on the initial screen
  • Aim to include permission for accessing the address as well

Research

Conducted research on other apps, categorising them based on the timing and methods used to obtain notification permissions.

Redesign the flow

Taking inspiration from the research,
Considered the following:

  • Compact the overall process to prevent drop-offs
  • Provide preliminary explanations before displaying pop-ups
  • Direct users to the permission screen after completing account registration
  • Introduce prompts for users who initially declined permission during onboarding

Revamp Design

Consolidated the permission page into a single screen,
made detailed adjustments, and created a unified onboarding process.
After implementation, the notification permission rate
saw a significant improvement, increasing from 46% to 68%.
